## What Does a Full-Stack Developer Actually Do?

A full-stack developer handles both the **front-end** (what users see and interact with) and the **back-end** (servers, databases, APIs, and business logic). Instead of needing a separate designer-developer-backend team, a good full-stack developer can take your idea from concept to a live, functional product.

For a Wollongong business, this often means:

- Building a custom web application or internal tool
- Modernising an outdated website or legacy system
- Integrating payment gateways, booking systems, or third-party APIs
- Setting up databases and cloud infrastructure (AWS, Heroku, Railway)
- Creating a customer-facing portal or e-commerce store

## What Should You Look for in a Wollongong Web Developer?

Here's a practical checklist when evaluating any developer, local or remote:

**Portfolio and past work**
Ask to see real projects they've built. Screenshots aren't enough. Look for live links or GitHub repositories you can review. Check whether their previous work matches the complexity of your project.

**Communication style**
A technically brilliant developer who can't explain things to a non-technical stakeholder will frustrate you. In your first conversation, notice whether they ask clarifying questions or jump straight to "I can do that."

**Full-stack vs specialist**
For most small-to-medium business projects, a full-stack developer offers better value than hiring separate frontend and backend specialists. The tradeoff is depth: a full-stack developer may not be the world's best at any single thing, but they're good at everything you need.

**Tech stack alignment**
Ask what technologies they use. In 2026, the modern web development stack for most projects looks like: **React.js or Next.js** (frontend), **Node.js with Express** (backend), **MongoDB or PostgreSQL** (database), and **AWS or similar cloud** (hosting). If a developer is still building in PHP without a framework or using dated tools, understand why.

**Client references or reviews**
Fiverr, Upwork, LinkedIn recommendations, or direct client testimonials are all valid. I have 50+ five-star reviews from global clients, and that track record means something.
